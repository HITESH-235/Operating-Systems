Definition: FCFS is a scheduling algo where the process that requests the CPU first is allocated the CPU first, hence a non-preemptive one

Advantages:
- Simplicity: Easy to understand and implement
- Fairness: Every process gets a chance to execute in the order they arrive
- No starvation: Every process will eventually get executed

Disadvantages:
- Long waiting time: If a long process arrives before shorter ones, it can lead to long waiting times for the shorter processes
- Poor performance: It can lead to poor CPU utilization and increased tat time, especially if there are processes with varying burst times
- Not suitable for time-sharing systems: 
    It does not allow for preemption, can lead to poor responsiveness in time-sharing systems where multiple processes need to share the CPU
